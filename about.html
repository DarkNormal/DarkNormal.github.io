About me

I was born in Dublin in '93 and spent most of my time between Dublin, Cork or Switzerland with my family. I've had a very keen interest in technology from the early age of 6 when I got my hands on my first original PlayStation. From this I progressed through a plethora of other devices and I'm still gathering more to this day. My first experience of programming was at 12 when I enrolled in Technology. Working with circuit boards, components and microcontrollers was a great experience, and set me straight towards studying Computer Science in the future. Much better than my aspiration of Bin-man at age 8.

I graduated secondary school with an award for academic excellence in Technology and recieved a total of 440 points in the Leaving Certificate. From there I was accepted into the Institute of Technology in Tallaght, Dublin where I would study Computing - a very practical and broad coure in comparison to other Computer Science courses. The major topics covered in the last 4 years include Software Development, Web Design and Development, Networking (in a certified Cisco environment, using physical components), Advanced Database Systems and Object Orientated System Analysis and Design.

While I was still in school, I got a part-time job in Glanbia working in a distribution warehouse. I started in 2011 and I quickly became one of the most consistent workers there. I usually worked weekday evenings and every Saturday up until September 2014 when I was pressed for further commitment. This would have interfered with my college studies so I decided on what was more important for my future.
I now host and maintain a multitude of websites, including designing new interfaces for individuals or businesses. I recently created a small Java application for a business which saved them at least €1500 in upfront-costs as well as significantly increased the efficiency of what was previously being used.

GitHub Pages

By committing all the website files to a GitHub repository, a website can be accessed using GitHub Pages. The repository must be called your_github_username.github.io, and this will allow the website to be created. This process is just as easy as using Azure, and somewhat better as it enables automatic source-control.
